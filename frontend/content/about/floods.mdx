## Overview

Floods occur when water overflows onto land that is normally dry. They are among the **most frequent natural disasters worldwide**, affecting millions of people each year. Floods can develop slowly over days or strike suddenly within minutes, especially during heavy rainfall or storm surges.

## Causes & Warning Signs

### Causes
- Heavy or prolonged rainfall  
- River or lake overflow  
- Rapid snowmelt  
- Storm surges in coastal areas  
- Dam or levee failure  

### Warning Signs
- Rising river or water levels  
- Continuous heavy rain  
- Flood alerts from authorities  
- Water pooling in unusual places  

---

## Preparedness

### Before a Flood
- Know your local flood risk and evacuation routes  
- Elevate electrical appliances and valuables  
- Keep important documents waterproofed  
- Prepare an emergency kit  

### During a Flood
- Move to higher ground immediately  
- Avoid walking or driving through floodwater  
- Follow evacuation orders without delay  

### After a Flood
- Avoid contaminated water  
- Check structures before entering buildings  
- Clean and disinfect affected areas
